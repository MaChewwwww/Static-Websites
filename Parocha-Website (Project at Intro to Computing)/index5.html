    About

    Started as petitions to have a higher educational institution for the youth at the National Government Center area from the community organizations and church groups and negotiations with local officials and other government agencies, the PUP Quezon City Campus is a pantheon of concrete success towards the struggle in people’s fight against ignorance. As enshrined on the Deed of Assignment on 24 March 1997, that is, “to establish a branch or an extension of PUP at the National Government Center with a mandate to bring quality education and subsidized tuition fees to the urban poor communities of Quezon City, with priority given to those from the NGCHP.” Since its establishment in 1997, PUP Quezon City continues to produce quality and globally competitive graduates in the areas of education, information technology, management, marketing, entrepreneurship and office management technology. Likewise, true to the University’s mandate as Pamantasan ng Bayan, its graduate degree thru the Open University education system increased by offering two additional programs including the first program offering as practical alternative venue from PUP Main Campus. And to seal its official existence as an affirmation of the University’s commitment to democratize access to educational opportunities for economically challenged students of society, Republic Act 11347, otherwise known as the "PUP Quezon City Campus Act" was signed into law on June 25, 2019 as a renewal of the same passion and commitment to "provide quality education to the urban poor communities of Quezon City and its neighboring localities."

Bachelor of Science in Information Technology
(BSIT)

<

A program that provides students with a comprehensive understanding of computer systems, networks, programming, and software development. It equips graduates with the skills needed to design, implement, and manage information technology solutions in various industries.

Bachelor of Science in Business Administration major in Marketing Management
(BSBA-MM)

A program that combines fundamental business principles with specialized coursework in marketing strategies, consumer behavior analysis, and market research methodologies, preparing graduates for careers in brand management, advertising, sales, and market analysis.

Bachelor of Science in Entrepreneurship
(BSENTREP)

A program designed to equip students with the knowledge and skills necessary to start, manage, and grow successful businesses. It encompasses courses in business planning, innovation, finance, marketing, and leadership, enabling graduates to navigate the complexities of entrepreneurship and pursue opportunities in various industries or launch their ventures effectively.

Bachelor of Public Administration major in Public Financial Management
(BPAPFM)

A program that integrates principles of public administration with specialized coursework focusing on financial planning, budgeting, and management within governmental organizations. It equips students with the necessary skills to effectively allocate public funds, assess fiscal policies, and ensure financial accountability in the public sector.

Bachelor of Science in Business Administration major in Human Resource Management
(BSBAHRM)

A program that blends core business principles with specialized coursework in personnel management, employee relations, recruitment, training, and organizational development. It prepares students to effectively manage human capital within organizations, fostering a productive and harmonious work environment while aligning HR strategies with overall business goals.

Bachelor of Business Technology and Livelihood Education major in Home Economics
(BBTLEDHE)

A program that combines business technology and education with a focus on home economics, covering areas such as culinary arts, textile and clothing technology, family resource management, and interior design. It prepares students for careers in teaching, entrepreneurship, or management roles related to domestic and consumer-focused industries.

Bachelor of Business Technology and Livelihood Education major in Information Communication and Technology
(BTLEDICT)

A program that combines business, technology, and education with a focus on ICT. It covers a range of subjects including computer science, information systems, business management, and educational methods tailored for teaching ICT skills. Graduates are prepared for careers in areas such as ICT education, technology training, educational technology development, and management positions that require a blend of business acumen and ICT expertise.

The Diploma in Office Management Technology with a focus on Medical Office Management
(DOMTMOM)

A specialized program designed to train individuals in administrative skills specifically tailored for medical settings. It covers areas such as medical terminology, healthcare laws and regulations, medical billing and coding, appointment scheduling, electronic health records management, and patient communication. Graduates are equipped with the knowledge and skills necessary to effectively manage the administrative aspects of medical offices, clinics, hospitals, or other healthcare facilities.

History

The PUP Quezon City Campus, formerly PUP Commonwealth Campus, is a 1.9-hectare forested land area and was assigned, transferred and endorsed to the Polytechnic University of the Philippines as ASSIGNEE by the Housing and Urban Development Coordinating Council-National Government Center Housing Committee (HUDCC-NGCHC), a government entity created by Executive Order No. 90 and with expanded functions under RA 7279 and Home Insurance And Guaranty Corporation (HIGC) as Assignor, a government owned and controlled corporation created under RA 580 and presently located at Don Fabian Street, Barangay Commonwealth, Quezon City. Thru the concerted efforts of Dr. Ofelia M. Carague, PUP Executive Vice President and Mr. Jaime E. Varela, Project Administrator, NGCHP, a Deed of Assignment was executed and signed by Atty. Dionisio C. Dela Serna, HUDCC-NGCHC Chairman, MR. Wilfredo F. Hernandez, HIGC President and DR. Zenaida A. Olonan, University President on 24 March 1997 with a sole objective to “establish a branch or an extension of PUP at the National Government Center with a mandate to bring quality education and subsidized tuition fees to the urban poor communities of Quezon City, with priority given to those from the NGCHP.”

Prior to this, an execution of Deed of Donation, whereby two (2) halls - Rothlehner and Misereor Building today in honor to their generosity and benevolence as funding-donors in which the two buildings were built - were donated to PUP for its purposes entered into by and between the Sentro sa Ikauunlad ng Kasanayan at Hanapbuhay (SIKHAY) – an association duly registered with the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) represented by its President, Rev. Fr. Joel E. Tabora, S.J. and PUP on 26 February 1997.

Upon its opening in 1997 offering a Master in Educational Management under the Open University System, PUP Quezon City also offered Bachelor of Science in Information Technology and a 1-Year Certificate in Information Communication Technology on its first year of formal operations during the Academic Year 1998-1999. As part of the donated property, the two original structures known as Rothlehner and Misereor Halls today became integral parts of the campus and now served as classrooms, Learning Resource Center, and academic and administrative offices for students and faculty members.

On July 29, 1997, PUP Quezon City was officially established during its formal launching on the very same grounds with the special presence of Mr. Rothlehner who himself was awarded the Plaque of Recognition by PUP President, Dr. Zenaida A. Olonan, together with other honored guests from the Local Government of Quezon City and PUP administration.

The PUP Quezon City Campus presently has a total of four buildings which include the newly constructed Academic Building with 12 classrooms and another one that houses the Audio-Visual Room and Computer Laboratory. In addition, an Ecumenical Chapel that can be converted to a mini hall and the Winnie Castelo Welfare Complex, a covered court that cater to the nonacademic needs of the students.

Considered as a miniature of the PUP Main Campus in its design, this “Green Campus” is a viable option for the Iskolar ng Bayan coming from northeast of Metro Manila and as far as Rizal and Bulacan as a haven for their search for knowledge. And to seal its official existence as an affirmation of the University’s commitment to democratize access to educational opportunities for economically challenged students of society, Republic Act 11347, otherwise known as the “PUP Quezon City Campus Act” was signed into law on June 25, 2019. This was made possible by the 17th Congress House Bill No. 7734 through the sponsorship of the 2nd District of Quezon City Representative, Honorable Winston “Winnie” T. Castelo in the House of Representatives as well as the Chair of Committee on Education, Arts and Culture, Honorable Francis “Chiz” G. Escudero from the Senate.

Started as petitions to have a higher educational institution for the youth at the National Government Center area from the community organizations and church groups, namely; Parokya ng Mabuting Pastol (PMP), Kaagapay sa Makatarungan at Pantay na Paninirahan, Inc. (KAMPI), SUPERBLOCKS, and Kristong Hari Foundation (KHF), and negotiations with local officials and other government agencies, the PUP Quezon City is a pantheon of concrete success towards the struggle in people’s fight against ignorance. The campus will continue its passion and commitment to “provide quality education to the urban poor communities of Quezon City and its neighboring localities.”

Faculty

Branch Director

The School Branch Director holds a pivotal role in providing visionary leadership and managing day-to-day operations within the branch school. This leadership encompasses crafting and articulating a clear mission and strategic direction in alignment with the overarching goals of the educational institution. Responsibilities include overseeing operational aspects such as staffing, budgeting, facilities management, and ensuring compliance with regulatory standards. Additionally, the Director recruits, trains, and evaluates teaching and administrative staff to maintain a collaborative work environment conducive to professional growth. Academic excellence remains a focal point, with the Director ensuring high-quality education delivery and program adherence to accreditation standards. Student support services are implemented under their guidance, addressing diverse needs including academic, social, and emotional support. Establishing strong relationships with parents, families, and the community is paramount, fostering engagement and support for the school's mission.

Office of Academic Programs

The Head of the Office of Academic Programs is responsible for overseeing all aspects of academic program development, implementation, and evaluation within the institution. This includes coordinating curriculum development, assessment, and accreditation efforts, as well as managing faculty and student support services. Additionally, they collaborate with various stakeholders to ensure alignment with institutional goals and standards, and may also be involved in strategic planning and resource allocation. Qualifications typically include a Master's or Doctoral degree in a relevant field, extensive experience in academic administration, strong leadership and communication skills, and a deep understanding of educational best practices and accreditation requirements.

Office of Administrative & Quality Management System

The Head of Administrative and Quality Management System oversees the development, implementation, and maintenance of administrative processes and quality management systems within the organization. This role involves coordinating administrative functions, ensuring compliance with regulatory standards, and optimizing operational efficiency. Additionally, the Head oversees quality assurance initiatives, conducts audits, and identifies opportunities for improvement. Qualifications typically include a Bachelor's or Master's degree in a relevant field, experience in administrative management or quality assurance, strong leadership and analytical skills, and a commitment to continuous improvement and excellence.

Office of the Students Affairs & Services

The Office of Student Affairs and Services is a crucial department within educational institutions, dedicated to supporting students' holistic development and well-being. Its core responsibilities include offering various services and programs aimed at fostering academic success, personal growth, and overall satisfaction among students. Typically, this office oversees areas such as counseling and mental health services, career guidance, student activities, housing, conduct policies, and health and wellness initiatives. Central to its mission is creating a supportive and inclusive campus environment conducive to student engagement and leadership development. It serves as a resource hub for students, faculty, and staff, providing guidance, advocacy, and support services to enhance the overall student experience. By addressing diverse student needs and interests, the Office of Student Affairs and Services plays a vital role in contributing to students' success and well-being during their educational journey.

OIC of Accounting Services

The Officer-in-Charge (OIC) of Accounting Services role oversees the financial management and reporting functions within the organization. Responsibilities typically include maintaining accurate financial records, preparing financial statements, managing accounts payable and receivable, and ensuring compliance with accounting principles and regulatory requirements. Additionally, the OIC may be involved in budgeting, forecasting, and financial analysis to support strategic decision-making. Strong analytical skills, attention to detail, and knowledge of accounting principles are essential for this position. The OIC of Accounting Services plays a critical role in providing timely and accurate financial information to support organizational goals and objectives.

Collecting & Disbursing Officer

The Collecting and Disbursing Officer role is responsible for managing the collection and disbursement of funds within the organization. Key responsibilities typically include overseeing the receipt of payments, processing invoices, reconciling accounts, and maintaining accurate financial records. Additionally, the officer ensures compliance with financial regulations and internal policies governing the collection and disbursement of funds. Strong organizational and numerical skills, attention to detail, and proficiency in financial software are essential for this position. The Collecting and Disbursing Officer plays a crucial role in ensuring the efficient and transparent management of financial transactions to support the organization's financial health and operations.

Branch Registrar

The Branch Registrar role is responsible for managing all aspects of student records and academic documentation within the branch school. Responsibilities typically include maintaining accurate student records, managing course registration processes, coordinating class schedules, and facilitating the graduation process. The Branch Registrar also ensures compliance with academic policies and regulations, manages transcript requests, and provides support to students, faculty, and staff regarding academic matters. Strong organizational skills, attention to detail, and knowledge of academic policies and procedures are essential for this position. The Branch Registrar plays a critical role in supporting the academic mission of the branch school by maintaining accurate records and facilitating smooth administrative processes related to student academic affairs.

In-charge of the Admissions Office

The In-charge of the Admissions Office role oversees all aspects of the admissions process within the institution. Responsibilities typically include developing and implementing admissions policies and procedures, coordinating recruitment efforts, reviewing applications, and making admission decisions. The In-charge also communicates with prospective students and their families, provides information about programs and admission requirements, and assists with the enrollment process. Strong communication and interpersonal skills, as well as knowledge of admissions procedures and educational programs, are essential for this role. The In-charge of the Admissions Office plays a pivotal role in attracting and admitting qualified students to the institution, contributing to its overall growth and success.

Guidance Counselor & Career Placement Office

The Guidance Counselor and Head of the Career and Placement Office role serves as a crucial resource for students in their academic and career development. Responsibilities include providing individual and group counseling to students on academic, personal, and career-related issues, conducting career assessments, and assisting students with career exploration and planning. Additionally, the counselor collaborates with faculty and staff to support students' academic success and personal growth. In the Career and Placement Office, responsibilities typically involve organizing career fairs, connecting students with internship and job opportunities, and providing resources for resume writing and interview preparation. Strong interpersonal skills, empathy, and knowledge of counseling techniques and career development theories are essential for this role. The Guidance Counselor and Head of the Career and Placement Office play a vital role in supporting students' transition from education to the workforce, helping them make informed decisions about their academic and career paths.

Quality Assurance & OJT Coordinator

The Quality Assurance and On-the-Job Training (OJT) Coordinator role holds responsibility for ensuring compliance with quality standards and facilitating effective OJT programs within the organization. Duties often include developing and implementing quality assurance processes, conducting audits and assessments, and identifying areas for improvement. Additionally, the coordinator coordinates OJT initiatives, liaises with internal departments and external partners to secure OJT placements, and evaluates program effectiveness. Strong organizational skills, attention to detail, and knowledge of quality management principles are essential for this position. The Quality Assurance and OJT Coordinator plays a critical role in maintaining quality standards and enhancing employee training and development opportunities within the organization. Moreover, the coordinator collaborates with stakeholders to ensure alignment between quality assurance practices and OJT objectives, fostering a culture of continuous improvement and professional development.

Faculty Researcher

The Faculty Researcher role involves conducting original research, publishing scholarly works, and contributing to the academic discourse within their field of expertise. Responsibilities typically include designing and executing research projects, securing funding, disseminating findings through publications and presentations, and mentoring students in research activities. The Faculty Researcher also collaborates with colleagues and external partners on interdisciplinary research initiatives and may participate in professional development activities to stay current with advancements in their field. Strong analytical skills, a solid publication record, and a passion for advancing knowledge are essential for this role. The Faculty Researcher plays a crucial role in advancing the institution's research agenda and enhancing its reputation as a center of academic excellence. Furthermore, the Faculty Researcher actively engages in grant writing and seeks external funding opportunities to support research endeavors and promote institutional research excellence. Additionally, the Faculty Researcher contributes to the academic community through peer review activities, conference participation, and serving on editorial boards, further enriching scholarly discourse and advancing the field.

Branch Physician

The Branch Physician role is responsible for providing medical care and support to students, faculty, and staff within the branch school. Responsibilities typically include conducting medical examinations, diagnosing illnesses, prescribing medications, and administering treatments for minor injuries and ailments. The Branch Physician also advises on health and wellness issues, promotes preventive care measures, and collaborates with other healthcare professionals as needed. Strong clinical skills, empathy, and a commitment to patient care are essential for this role. The Branch Physician plays a vital role in promoting the health and well-being of the school community, ensuring a safe and supportive learning environment for all. Additionally, the Branch Physician may organize health education programs and workshops to raise awareness about common health issues and promote healthy lifestyle choices among students and staff. They also maintain accurate medical records and ensure compliance with healthcare regulations to uphold the highest standards of medical care within the branch school.

Laboratory Head & ICTO Coordinator

The Laboratory Head and ICTO Coordinator role oversees the operation and management of laboratory facilities while also coordinating Information and Communication Technology (ICT) initiatives within the organization. Responsibilities include ensuring the proper functioning of laboratory equipment, maintaining safety protocols, and overseeing the procurement of supplies. Additionally, the coordinator facilitates the integration of ICT tools and resources into academic and administrative activities, provides technical support to users, and promotes the effective use of technology for teaching, learning, and research purposes. Strong leadership skills, technical expertise in laboratory management and ICT systems, and a commitment to innovation are essential for this role. The Laboratory Head and ICTO Coordinator play a pivotal role in supporting both scientific research endeavors and technological advancements within the organization, fostering a conducive environment for academic excellence and technological innovation.

OIC of Scholarship & Financial Assistance

The Officer-in-Charge (OIC) of Scholarship and Financial Assistance role oversees the administration and distribution of scholarships and financial aid programs within the institution. Responsibilities include managing scholarship applications, assessing eligibility criteria, and disbursing awards to deserving students. Additionally, the OIC develops and implements policies and procedures related to financial assistance programs, provides guidance and support to students in navigating scholarship opportunities, and liaises with external organizations and donors to secure funding. Strong organizational skills, attention to detail, and a commitment to promoting access to education are essential for this role. The OIC of Scholarship and Financial Assistance plays a crucial role in supporting students' academic pursuits by providing financial support and reducing financial barriers to higher education.

Collecting Officer

The Collecting Officer role is responsible for managing the collection of payments and fees within the organization. Responsibilities typically include receiving payments from customers or clients, issuing receipts, and maintaining accurate records of transactions. Additionally, the Collecting Officer may handle inquiries related to billing and payments, process refunds or adjustments, and ensure compliance with financial regulations and internal policies. Strong attention to detail, numerical proficiency, and excellent customer service skills are essential for this role. The Collecting Officer plays a critical role in ensuring the efficient and accurate processing of financial transactions, contributing to the organization's financial stability and operational effectiveness.

Acting Security Officer

The Acting Security Officer role assumes temporary responsibility for overseeing security operations within the organization. Duties typically include implementing security protocols, conducting security assessments, and coordinating with external security agencies if needed. Additionally, the Acting Security Officer may handle incidents or emergencies, enforce safety policies, and provide guidance to staff on security procedures. Strong leadership skills, crisis management abilities, and a commitment to maintaining a safe and secure environment are essential for this role. The Acting Security Officer plays a crucial role in safeguarding the organization's assets, personnel, and premises during their temporary tenure in this position.
